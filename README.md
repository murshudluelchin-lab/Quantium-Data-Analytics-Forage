# Quantium Data Analytics Job Simulation — Forage

## Overview
This repository contains my work for the Quantium Data Analytics 
Job Simulation on Forage. The simulation focused on analyzing 
supermarket chip sales data to derive customer insights and 
evaluate trial store performance.

**Tools used:** Power BI, Power Query, DAX, PowerPoint  
**Completed:** May 2026  
**Certificate:** [View Certificate](./Quantium_Data_Analytics_Certificate_Elchin_Murshudlu.pdf)

---

## Task 1 — Data Preparation & Customer Analytics
- Cleaned and analyzed transaction and customer data
- Identified top customer segments by total sales and behavior
- Key finding: Budget Older Families ($156.9K) and Mainstream 
  Young Singles/Couples ($147.6K) are the highest revenue-generating segments
- Kettle dominates brand sales with $390K; 175g is the most preferred pack size

---

## Task 2 — Experimentation & Uplift Testing
- Identified Control Stores for each Trial Store based on 
  Pre-Trial sales and customer count similarity
- Trial Stores: 77, 86, 88
- Control Stores: 53, 73, 65

| Trial Store | Control Store |
|-------------|---------------|
| Store 77    | Store 53      |
| Store 86    | Store 73      |
| Store 88    | Store 65      |

- Compared Pre-Trial, Trial (Feb–Apr 2019) and Post-Trial periods
- Trial Store 88 demonstrated the strongest sales uplift

---

## Task 3 — Analytics & Commercial Application
- Prepared a strategic report for the Category Manager (Julia)
  using the Pyramid Principles framework
- Report includes customer insights, brand analysis, 
  trial store results and recommendations

---

## Key Recommendations
1. Focus marketing on Budget Older Families and Mainstream 
   Young Singles/Couples
2. Prioritize Kettle and Smiths in 175g and 150g pack sizes
3. Roll out the new store layout based on Trial Store 88 results
